The Problem

Good Food Goes to Waste While Families Go Hungry

Every year in the United States, over 80 million tons of food end up in landfills — much of it still fresh and perfectly edible. At the same time, 1 in 8 families struggles to put a meal on the table. The disconnect isn't about supply; it's about logistics. Restaurants toss unsold meals at closing. Grocery stores discard produce that didn't sell by its shelf date. Farms leave imperfect crops in the field.

The food is there. The need is there. What's missing is the bridge — a fast, reliable system to rescue surplus and deliver it to people who need it before it spoils.

Our Journey

From a Student Idea to a Community Lifeline

What began as a computer science project to track food waste became a full-fledged organization rescuing thousands of meals every week.

The Spark

Jane Doe noticed that local restaurants were throwing away trays of untouched food every night. As a computer science student, she believed technology could close the gap between surplus and need.

The Prototype

She built a mobile app that allowed restaurant managers to flag available surplus with one tap. Volunteers nearby would receive an alert and pick up the food within the hour.

First Partnerships

Local grocery stores and farms saw the potential and joined. Fresh produce that would have been composted instead found its way to shelters and community fridges.

HarvestLoop Is Born

With growing demand and a proven model, the project formally launched as HarvestLoop — a nonprofit dedicated to creating a continuous loop of rescued food flowing to those who need it most.

What We Do

Programs That Keep Food in the Loop

Each initiative tackles a different link in the chain from surplus to table.

Rescue

The Rescue Loop

Our same-day pickup network uses real-time alerts to match surplus meals from restaurants and caterers with volunteer drivers who deliver directly to shelters — often within 90 minutes of the food being flagged.

Partnerships

FreshCraft Partnerships

We work with local farms and community gardens to channel imperfect but nutritious produce directly into our distribution network — bypassing the cosmetic standards that lead to farm-level waste.

Technology

LoopConnect App

Our mobile platform is the nerve center of everything we do. Donors list surplus in seconds, volunteers claim pickups on a live map, and shelters track incoming deliveries — all in real time.

Community

SeedStart Kitchen Circles

Weekly gatherings where families learn to prepare nutritious meals from rescued ingredients. Led by local chefs, these sessions build cooking confidence and reduce repeat food waste at the household level.

Our Founder

Meet Jane Doe

"I kept watching perfectly good food go into dumpsters while people a few blocks away went to bed hungry. I couldn't just watch — I had to build something."

Jane Doe — Founder & Executive Director

Jane is a computer science student who channeled her technical skills into solving one of her community's most urgent problems. What started as a class project to map food waste patterns grew into a real-time rescue network that now operates across multiple neighborhoods.

She designed and built the LoopConnect app from scratch, personally recruited the first 50 volunteer drivers, and forged partnerships with over 30 local restaurants and grocery stores. Jane believes that technology should serve human needs first — and HarvestLoop is her proof of concept.
